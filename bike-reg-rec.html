Background

The Background

During my work at Kellermann Lighting GmbH & Co. KG, I got my hands on some samples of the Atto WL and Atto Integral RB position and rear lights with deviating driver boards.
The Atto series are exceptionally small lights that complain and exceed with German road legality standards. Their unmatched size makes them an ideal candidate for seamless integration onto a bicycle.
The current smallest bicycle (rear) light is the Busch & Müller [my:] , which still exceeds the Atto models in size.

With the Atto being made for DC motorcycle circuits, they are not simply to be connected to the dynamo hub. This sparked the idea of a small AC-DC system for bicyles.

The ideas kept coming

Extending the "simple" idea of a AC-DC converter, some more attributes got into my head.
During long distance off grid bicycle touring, power management can be an issue. With the accessibility of hub dynamos this issue could be resolved.
The average dynamo hub outputs 3 Watts at 6 Volt AC.

There are several examples of AC-DC convertors already available. Some examples are the Forumslader , the Cycle2Charge , or the Sinewave Reactor . Yet, those only output 5V to charge a phone or similar device.
Some even contain buffer batteries and some are more integrated into the bicycle than others. Furthermore in my ideology, bicycles are not a luxury good. I do understand the costs of R&D, quality control, supply networks and such, but I think that a more affordable solution can be done.

The Goals

The aim of this project ist to produce a system that integrates well into the bicycle. This system has to check the following boxes:
    Buffer battery
    5V output to charge devices
    >6V output for DC lights
    A toggle to switch between operation modes
    Affordability for the average student
    Ease of installation

Development

Development

A prototype version consisting of a simple cicruit of a diode rectifier and capacitors is currently mounted to a bicycle with a Shutter Precision PD-8 dynamo hub.
The prototype supplies sufficient Watts to power two Kellermann Atto on each end of the bicycle.
